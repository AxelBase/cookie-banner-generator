Google Analytics Is Dead – Here’s What to Use in 2025

Published: November 1, 2025

For years, Google Analytics was the default choice for tracking website performance. But in 2025, it’s no longer viable for many SMBs. Privacy regulations, cookie consent requirements, and technical complexity have made it more of a liability than a solution. The good news? There are privacy-safe alternatives that deliver actionable insights without requiring consent banners.

Why Google Analytics Fell Behind

GDPR and CCPA made cookie-based tracking harder to justify. Customers increasingly reject consent banners, leaving businesses with incomplete data. Google’s reliance on personal identifiers has become a compliance headache.

Better Alternatives

Modern analytics tools focus on privacy-first tracking. They use aggregated, anonymized data to provide insights without storing personal information. This means you can measure traffic, conversions, and engagement without worrying about fines or customer backlash.

Recommended Tools

  • Plausible Analytics – lightweight, open-source, and cookie-free.
  • Fathom Analytics – simple dashboards with strong privacy guarantees.
  • Matomo – self-hosted option for full control over your data.

FAQ

Do I still need a cookie banner?

No, these tools don’t require consent because they don’t track personal data.

Are they as powerful as Google Analytics?

For SMBs, yes. They cover the metrics that matter most without unnecessary complexity.

Is setup difficult?

Most tools offer simple installation, often just a single script tag.

Analytics should empower, not burden. Privacy-first tools are the future of SMB data tracking.
